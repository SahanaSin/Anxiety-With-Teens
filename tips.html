TIPS


  • Prioritize self-care
    • Science proves that pritoritizing self-care makes you better handle anxiety triggers.

  • Establish a routine
    • This provides structure, stability and a sense of control.

  • Regular exercise
    • Exercise helps release endorphins (which reduce stress and improve your mood).

  • Better thoughts
    • Our expectations are often what we expect. For example, a video had gone viral over social media where someone speaks without sound. Although, the audience is influenced by the contents in the video to hear sounds and words that were never spoken. This is because our brain wants us to believe something is real by having a sort of answer to it. This is better known as the auditory illusion, McGurk Effect science experiment.

  • Sufficient sleep
    • Sleep relaxes your mind and body.

  • Balanced diet
    • A balanced diet provides the necessary nutrients for your brain's health.

  • Engaging in activities that bring joy and/or relaxation
    • This can distract anxious thoughts from your mind.

  • Meditation
    • Meditation is provided as a distraction, mood booster, self-care engine and relaxer.

  • Deep breathing exercises
    • Deep breathing helps activate the body's relaxation response.

  • Supportive friends and family
    • Sometimes all you simply need is someone comfortable who you can talk to.

  • Engaging in activities that promote self-expression
    • It allows you to express yourself and find a sense of inner peace.

  • Re-evaluate commitments
    • If life is overwhelming, it’s okay to let go of commitments for a while. It’s important to take care of yourself.

  • Set healthy boundaries















It’s important to remember that everyone is different. There’s no right way to deal with stress. Be patient and explore coping strategies and find that work best for you.
